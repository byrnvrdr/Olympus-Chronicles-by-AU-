Apollo ‚Äî God of Light, Music, and Prophecy

Apollo, the radiant son of Zeus and Leto, embodies harmony and brilliance. As the god of the Sun, he drives his golden chariot across the sky each day. Known for his unmatched skill with the lyre and bow, Apollo guided humanity through art, healing, and divine wisdom. His oracle at Delphi spoke the will of the gods, shaping destinies for mortals and kings alike.

Zeus ‚Äî King of the Gods, Lord of the Sky

Zeus reigns from the heights of Olympus, wielding thunder as both weapon and judgment. He overthrew his father Cronus and the Titans to bring order to chaos. Protector of law and oaths, he rewards the just and strikes down the arrogant. From the clouds he governs gods and mortals alike, a symbol of both mercy and power.

Athena ‚Äî Goddess of Wisdom and Strategy

Born fully armed from the head of Zeus, Athena represents intellect joined with courage. Patron of Athens, she inspired philosophers, artisans, and warriors to use reason before force. With her owl as companion and aegis shield in hand, she defends justice and civilization. Calm yet unstoppable, she is wisdom given form.

Poseidon ‚Äî Lord of the Seas and Earthquakes

Brother to Zeus and Hades, Poseidon rules the depths where sunlight fades. With his mighty trident he can raise islands or shatter them beneath raging waves. Sailors prayed to him for calm winds, yet feared his storms. Beneath his realm lie coral palaces, drowned cities, and the restless spirits of the sea.

Hades ‚Äî Lord of the Underworld

The silent ruler of the realm of the dead, Hades governs the afterlife with fairness and resolve. Though feared by mortals, he is not evil ‚Äî his duty is to maintain balance between life and death. Guarded by Cerberus, his kingdom thrives in the shadows where souls find eternal rest.

Artemis ‚Äî Goddess of the Moon and the Hunt

Twin sister of Apollo, Artemis roams the night forests under silver light. She protects the wild and all who wander it, swift with her bow and fierce in her independence. To hunters and maidens she offers freedom and grace; to the proud she is a merciless judge. The moon itself is her torch, guiding her eternal chase across the stars.
